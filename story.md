
# The Beltline Effect: Race, Space, and Neighborhood Transformation in Atlanta

## Executive Summary

Atlanta has long been recognized as a center of Black culture, wealth, and community, often referred to as the “Black Mecca.” This project examines how that identity is shifting in the context of the Atlanta BeltLine, one of the largest urban redevelopment projects in the United States. Using census tract data from 2000, 2010, and 2020, I analyze changes in median income, housing costs, and racial composition in relation to proximity to the BeltLine.

The key finding is that neighborhoods closest to the BeltLine—many of which were historically Black—experienced the most dramatic changes. Median income increased by over 145%, rent more than doubled, and the Black population declined significantly while the White population increased. These patterns suggest that redevelopment has not been evenly experienced and is closely tied to processes of gentrification and displacement.
